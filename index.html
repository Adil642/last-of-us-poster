Look For The Light

I’ve seen the world end quietly — not with fire or war, but with the slow silence of people forgetting how to care. I’ve watched cities rot, buildings swallowed by vines, and laughter fade like dust in the wind. But even in that ruin, I found something worth saving. It wasn’t the cure. It wasn’t the mission. It was her. A girl who talked to herself, who read bad jokes off torn pages, who was stubborn enough to believe in something better. She made the dead world feel alive again. And for that, I would carry every sin, every lie, every scar. Because love, in a world like this, isn’t a gift — it’s a rebellion.
